What is Artificial Intelligence?
The big picture — defining AI in plain English
🔒
⌄

What You'll Learn

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the ability of a computer or machine to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. These tasks include understanding language, recognizing images, making decisions, and learning from experience.

A Simple Definition

Think of AI as teaching a computer to think — not by programming every single rule, but by showing it lots of examples so it figures out the patterns itself.

Key Facts

  • AI is not magic — it's math and data working together
  • AI systems learn from examples, just like humans learn from experience
  • AI can be narrow (good at one thing) or general (good at many things)
  • Most AI today is narrow AI — designed for specific tasks

Real World Examples You Already Use

  • Netflix recommending what to watch next
  • Google autocompleting your searches
  • Face unlock on your phone
  • Spam filters in your email
  • Google Maps predicting traffic

Key Takeaway

AI is not science fiction. It's a tool — one you can learn to use and even build yourself. That's exactly what this course is about.

History of AI
From Turing to ChatGPT — the journey so far
🔒
⌄

The Beginning

AI didn't start with ChatGPT. The idea goes back to 1950 when mathematician Alan Turing asked: 'Can machines think?' He invented the Turing Test — a way to measure if a machine could hold a conversation indistinguishable from a human.

Key Milestones

  • 1950 — Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test
  • 1956 — The term 'Artificial Intelligence' is coined at Dartmouth College
  • 1997 — IBM Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov
  • 2011 — IBM Watson wins Jeopardy! against human champions
  • 2012 — Deep learning breakthrough with ImageNet competition
  • 2016 — AlphaGo defeats world Go champion
  • 2022 — ChatGPT reaches 100 million users in 2 months

AI Winters

AI didn't grow in a straight line. There were periods called 'AI winters' where funding dried up and progress slowed — because expectations were too high and computers weren't powerful enough yet.

Key Takeaway

We are now living in the most exciting period in AI history — and it's only getting started.

Types of AI
Narrow AI, General AI, and Superintelligence
🔒
⌄

The 3 Types

  • Narrow AI (ANI) — Designed for one specific task. ALL the AI we have today.
  • General AI (AGI) — Can perform any intellectual task a human can. Does not exist yet.
  • Superintelligence (ASI) — Smarter than the best human in every field. Theoretical only.

Narrow AI Examples

  • ChatGPT — great at text, can't drive a car
  • DALL-E — great at images, can't write code
  • AlphaFold — great at protein structures, nothing else
  • Siri — great at voice commands, limited reasoning

By Learning Approach

  • Supervised Learning — Learns from labelled examples
  • Unsupervised Learning — Finds patterns in unlabelled data
  • Reinforcement Learning — Learns by trial and error with rewards

Key Takeaway

Focus on Narrow AI — that's what exists, what's useful, and what you can actually build with right now.

How Machine Learning Works
The engine behind modern AI explained simply
🔒
⌄

What is Machine Learning?

Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI where computers learn from data without being explicitly programmed for every task. Instead of writing rules, you feed the machine examples and it figures out the rules itself.

The Learning Process

  • Step 1: Collect data (thousands or millions of examples)
  • Step 2: Feed data to the algorithm
  • Step 3: The algorithm finds patterns
  • Step 4: The model makes predictions on new data
  • Step 5: Measure accuracy and improve

Simple Analogy

Imagine teaching a child to recognize cats. You don't explain 'four legs, whiskers, fur.' You show them 1000 pictures of cats and 1000 of non-cats. After enough examples, they can identify a cat they've never seen before. Machine Learning works the same way.

Training vs Inference

  • Training — The learning phase (expensive, done once)
  • Inference — Using the trained model to make predictions (fast, done many times)

Neural Networks & Deep Learning
How AI mimics the human brain
🔒
⌄

What is a Neural Network?

A neural network is a system loosely inspired by the human brain. It consists of layers of connected nodes (neurons) that process information and pass it forward to produce an output.

The Structure

  • Input Layer — Receives raw data (e.g. pixels of an image)
  • Hidden Layers — Process and transform the data
  • Output Layer — Produces the final result (e.g. 'this is a cat')

What is Deep Learning?

Deep Learning is just a neural network with many hidden layers — making it 'deep.' More layers = ability to learn more complex patterns. This powers image recognition, voice assistants, language models, and self-driving cars.

Why Did Deep Learning Explode?

Three things happened simultaneously: massive datasets became available, GPUs made training fast enough, and better algorithms were discovered. The combination unlocked a revolution.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)
How AI understands and generates human language
🔒
⌄

What is NLP?

Natural Language Processing is the branch of AI that deals with understanding, interpreting, and generating human language. It's what makes AI able to read, write, and converse.

NLP Tasks

  • Text Classification — Is this email spam?
  • Sentiment Analysis — Is this review positive?
  • Translation — Google Translate
  • Summarization — Condense a long article
  • Question Answering — ChatGPT, Claude
  • Text Generation — Writing essays, code, emails

How Does AI Understand Language?

AI converts words into numbers called embeddings and finds mathematical relationships between them. Words with similar meanings end up with similar numbers.

Large Language Models (LLMs)

LLMs like Claude and GPT are trained on billions of words from the internet. They learn to predict the next word in a sentence — and through this, they develop surprisingly deep language abilities.

Computer Vision
How AI sees and interprets the visual world
🔒
⌄

What is Computer Vision?

Computer Vision enables machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world — images, videos, and live camera feeds.

What Can Computer Vision Do?

  • Image Classification — What is in this photo?
  • Object Detection — Where are the objects in this image?
  • Facial Recognition — Who is this person?
  • Medical Imaging — Detecting cancer in X-rays
  • OCR — Reading text from images

How Does It Work?

An image is just a grid of pixels, each with a colour value. Neural networks process these pixel values layer by layer — first detecting edges, then shapes, then high-level features like eyes or wheels.

Real World Impact

Computer vision is saving lives in medicine, enabling autonomous vehicles, and powering quality control in factories.

AI Ethics & Bias
The responsibilities that come with building AI
🔒
⌄

Why Ethics Matters in AI

AI systems make decisions that affect people's lives — loan approvals, job applications, medical diagnoses. If those systems are biased or unfair, the consequences are real and serious.

What is AI Bias?

AI learns from historical data. If that data reflects human prejudice, the AI will too.

Real Examples of Bias

  • Facial recognition performing worse on darker skin tones
  • Hiring algorithms favouring male candidates
  • Loan systems disadvantaging certain zip codes
  • Medical AI trained mostly on Western populations

Key Ethical Principles

  • Fairness — AI should treat all groups equitably
  • Transparency — Users should know when AI is making decisions
  • Privacy — Personal data must be protected
  • Accountability — Someone must be responsible for AI decisions
  • Safety — AI must not cause harm

AI in Africa & the Developing World
Opportunities, challenges, and your role
🔒
⌄

The African AI Opportunity

Africa has the youngest and fastest-growing population in the world. Combined with rapid mobile adoption, this creates an enormous opportunity for AI to solve uniquely African problems.

Where AI is Making an Impact

  • Fintech — AI-powered credit scoring for the unbanked (OPay, Moniepoint)
  • Agriculture — Crop disease detection using phone cameras
  • Healthcare — Diagnosing malaria and TB from images
  • Education — Personalised learning in local languages

The Challenges

  • Limited local training data in African languages
  • Infrastructure gaps (power, internet)
  • Most AI tools built for Western contexts
  • Brain drain — talent leaving the continent

Your Role

You are part of the generation that will build African AI. The problems are real, the market is massive, and the tools have never been more accessible.

The Future of AI
What's coming and how to stay ahead
🔒
⌄

Where AI is Heading

  • Multimodal AI — Systems that see, hear, and speak simultaneously
  • AI Agents — AI that takes actions autonomously on your behalf
  • Personal AI — AI assistants that know you and improve over time
  • Edge AI — Powerful AI running directly on phones and devices

Jobs and AI

Will AI take jobs? Some yes, but it will also create new ones. The most important skill is learning to work WITH AI — using it to multiply your productivity.

How to Stay Ahead

  • Keep learning — AI moves fast, you must too
  • Build things — the best way to understand AI is to use it
  • Think about problems — AI is a tool, problems are what matter
